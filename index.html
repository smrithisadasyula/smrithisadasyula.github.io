About Me

  • Name: Smrithi Sadasyula
  • Age: 21 Years
  • Residence: India
  • Hometown: Hyderabad

Choosing filmmaking and audio-visual production as my specialization was a no-brainer for me. As an ardent film enthusiast, particularly drawn to the charm of coming-of-age romances, I find immense joy in dissecting movies on Letterboxd. For me, filmmaking isn't just a career path; it's a passion, a calling, and a dream come true. Each frame tells a story, and being able to create and craft those narratives is where I find true fulfillment and creative expression.

It's not just about making movies; it's about immersing myself in the magic of storytelling and bringing dreams to life on the screen.

    The Hands That Feed

    "The Hands That Feed" is a heartwarming documentary that sheds light on the unsung heroes in our neighbourhoods who selflessly care for stray dogs. Through poignant interviews and captivating footage, we witness the daily acts of kindness and compassion as these good Samaritans provide food, shelter, and love to our furry friends. Join us on a journey of hope, community, and the enduring bond between humans and animals in our urban landscapes.

    Fine Kettle of Fish

    In a dystopian world plagued by water scarcity, power and influence dictate survival. Adya and Bhavya, an upper-middle-class couple, grapple with daily water struggles alongside their house help, Esha. Nimisha and Uday, a politically connected duo controlling water distribution, manipulate and exploit them. Tensions escalate, culminating in a revealing confrontation, exposing the true cost of power and privilege. As the film concludes, the characters' choices shape their diverging fates, highlighting the harsh realities of a society driven by greed and disparity.

    Maalish

    The film "Maalish" portrays Mona's journey through the challenges of early motherhood. Mona, meticulous by nature, struggles with the unpredictability and demands of caring for her child. Despite her efforts, she grapples with feelings of inadequacy and the fear of losing her identity. Her supportive husband, Rohan, and her mother provide some solace amidst her doubts. The story highlights the universal experience of young mothers grappling with self-doubt and the comforting presence of familial support.
